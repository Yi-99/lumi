The Voynich Manuscript

The Voynich manuscript is an illustrated codex written in an otherwise unknown script, referred to as Voynichese. The vellum on which it is written has been carbon-dated to the early fifteenth century, and stylistic analysis suggests it may have been composed in Italy during the Renaissance.

Many hypotheses about its meaning have been proposed: an undeciphered natural language, an elaborate cipher, a constructed language, or simply a hoax designed to defraud a wealthy collector. Statistical analysis shows the text follows Zipf's law, a property of natural languages that is difficult to fake, which keeps cryptographers returning to it.

The manuscript's botanical illustrations depict plants that match no known species, and its astronomical diagrams include zodiac symbols alongside inscrutable circular charts. It now resides in Yale University's Beinecke Rare Book & Manuscript Library.
